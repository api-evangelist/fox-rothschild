Examines the North Carolina Court of Appeals case State v. Dade, where a district court's failure to enter written findings and conclusions on a suppression motion in an implied-consent driving offense created a jurisdictional deficiency that rendered all subsequent proceedings void. Criminal practitioners should ensure district courts enter written determinations on suppression motions, and appellate practitioners should file certiorari petitions when facing jurisdictional uncertainty.
